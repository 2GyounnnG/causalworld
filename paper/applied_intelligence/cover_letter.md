# Cover Letter

Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Stability-Dependent Latent Prior Geometry for Structured World Models," for consideration in Applied Intelligence.

This paper studies latent prior geometry for structured world models, with an emphasis on long-horizon rollout stability. We compare no prior, Euclidean regularization, and spectral graph priors in molecular and synthetic structured domains. The central result is that spectral priors can strongly improve structured molecular rollout when aligned with relational dynamics, but they are not universally superior. In a completed Wolfram flat boundary case, Euclidean regularization has the best H=16 mean, while the spectral prior exhibits heavy-tailed long-horizon instability.

We believe the manuscript fits Applied Intelligence because it addresses practical design choices in intelligent predictive systems: how to regularize latent spaces, how to evaluate long-horizon stability, and how to avoid overclaiming from short-horizon metrics. The paper is not positioned as a pure theory contribution or as a refutation of any specific prior architecture. Instead, it provides an applied empirical analysis with explicit boundary cases and limitations.

The submission makes no universal claim that spectral priors dominate Euclidean priors. Rather, it argues that latent prior geometry is domain-specific and stability-dependent. We hope this framing will be useful to researchers building structured world models for molecular, physical, and graph-structured prediction tasks.

Sincerely,

The authors
